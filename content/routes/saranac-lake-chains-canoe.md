# Saranac Lake Chains Canoe Route

## Overview
Scenic paddle through the Lower, Middle, and Upper Saranac Lake chain with classic island campsites and short carries.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
20 miles paddling, ~1 mile carrying; negligible elevation gain.

## Difficulty
Moderate; open water crossings can be windy but carries are short.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Launch at South Creek on Middle Saranac and paddle north through the locks to Lower Saranac.
- Camp on designated island sites; reserve through the DEC system in peak season.
- Exit at Upper Saranac via Bartlett Carry with a brief portage.

## Tips
- Practice lock etiquette at the Upper Locks; have a painter line ready.
- Early starts avoid afternoon winds.
- Bring a map showing campsite numbers for easy navigation.

## Bailouts
- Multiple public launches on Lower Saranac allow flexible exits.
- Bartlett Carry puts you near Route 3 for pickup if needed.

## Weather Considerations
- Afternoon thunderstorms are common in summer; plan to be off exposed water.
- Cold shoulder-season water warrants PFD use and spare dry layers.
