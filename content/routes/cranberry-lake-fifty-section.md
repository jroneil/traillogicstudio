# Cranberry Lake 50 Lakeside Section

## Overview
Two-day backpacking sampler of the Cranberry Lake 50 focusing on the scenic lakeside segments with easy navigation and mellow grades.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
24 miles, ~1,200 feet gain.

## Difficulty
Moderate; mostly flat but long miles with roots and mud.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Start at the Cranberry Lake campground and hike clockwise along the lake shore.
- Camp at designated sites near Dog Pond or Olmstead Pond for a quiet night.
- Finish at the Peavine Swamp trailhead with a short road walk/shuttle.

## Tips
- Carry a detailed CL50 map for campsite locations.
- Water is abundant; filter from ponds and streams.
- Spring and fall offer fewer bugs and cooler temps.

## Warnings
- Mud and standing water common; bring gaiters.
- Expect minimal cell coverage—carry a backup navigation tool.

## Bailouts
- Several road crossings allow staged exits, including Chair Rock Flow and Curtis Pond Road.

## Weather Considerations
- Heavy rain can swell small creeks; check forecasts before committing.
- Cold nights arrive early in shoulder seasons—pack a warm sleep system.
