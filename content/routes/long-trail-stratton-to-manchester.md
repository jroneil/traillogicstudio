# Long Trail: Stratton to Manchester Center

## Overview
Classic southern Long Trail stretch passing Stratton Mountain and Bromley with convenient access to services in Manchester.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
17 miles, ~3,500 feet gain.

## Difficulty
Moderate; well-marked trail with steady climbs and good shelter spacing.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Start at Kelley Stand Road and climb Stratton, visiting the fire tower for views.
- Overnight at Stratton Pond Shelter for a relaxed pace.
- Continue over Styles Peak and Bromley, then descend the ski trail to Route 11/30 and hitch to town.

## Warnings
- Wet bog bridges near Stratton Pond can be slick.
- Expect heavy weekend traffic near the pond and Bromley.

## Bailouts
- Stratton-Arlington Road near Stratton Pond provides an early exit.
- Bromley ski area lifts/roads allow quick descent if weather turns.

## Weather Considerations
- Fog can obscure the Stratton tower views; check forecasts for clarity.
- Winter travel requires snowshoes under Green Mountain NF rules.
