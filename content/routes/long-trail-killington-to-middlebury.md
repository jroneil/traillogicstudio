# Long Trail: Killington to Middlebury Gap

## Overview
Multi-day Long Trail segment moving north from Killington Peak through airy ridgelines, remote shelters, and the Brandon Gap high country.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
45 miles, ~8,500 feet gain.

## Difficulty
Strenuous backpack of 3–4 days with rolling terrain and rugged tread.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Day 1: Sherburne Pass to Pico Camp and Killington spur, overnight at Rolston Rest.
- Day 2: Continue past Chittenden Brook and camp near David Logan Shelter.
- Day 3: Traverse the Breadloaf Wilderness and finish at Middlebury Gap; add a fourth day for shorter mileage.

## Tips
- Water sources are plentiful but can dry late season—carry extra in dry spells.
- Bear boxes exist at some shelters but bring a hang kit as backup.
- Hitching from Middlebury Gap is common; plan a shuttle ahead.

## Warnings
- Remote Breadloaf section sees limited maintenance; expect blowdowns.
- Mud and slick roots prevalent even in summer.

## Bailouts
- Forest roads near Brandon Gap provide exit options.
- Chittenden Brook Trail offers an early loop out to a campground.

## Weather Considerations
- Afternoon storms can be intense on the higher ridges; plan shelter stops accordingly.
- Cold nights possible even in summer at higher elevations—pack layers.
