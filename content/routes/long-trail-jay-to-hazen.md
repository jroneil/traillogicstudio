# Long Trail: Jay Peak to Hazen's Notch

## Overview
Northern Long Trail stretch near the Canadian border with airy Jay Peak views and quiet forest between ski areas.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
13 miles, ~3,500 feet gain.

## Difficulty
Moderate-strenuous day hike or easy overnight with steep grades near the peaks.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Start at Route 242 and climb Jay Peak early to beat gondola crowds.
- Continue north over Doll Peak and Burnt Mountain to Hazen's Notch Road.
- Overnight option at Laura Woodward Shelter to split the day.

## Tips
- Stop at the summit snack bar if open for water and treats.
- Cell service is decent near the ski area but fades quickly—download maps offline.
- Trail is well blazed; look for signs where ski runs intersect.

## Warnings
- Steep, rocky descents from Jay can be slippery when wet.
- Expect mud and roots typical of the northern LT.

## Bailouts
- Jay Peak resort access roads provide exits if weather turns.
- Hazen's Notch Road is seasonally maintained; confirm pickup access.

## Weather Considerations
- Ridge often windy; pack layers even in summer.
- Winter conditions can linger late—microspikes or snowshoes may be needed in spring.
