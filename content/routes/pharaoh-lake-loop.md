# Pharaoh Lake Loop

## Overview
Mellow circuit around Pharaoh Lake in the eastern Adirondacks with sandy beaches, lean-tos, and frequent water access.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
11 miles, ~900 feet gain.

## Difficulty
Easy-moderate; suitable for families and newer backpackers.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Start at the main Pharaoh Lake Trailhead and hike counterclockwise to hit lean-tos on the west shore.
- Spend the night at a lakeside site, then complete the loop via the east shore.
- Optional side trip up Pharaoh Mountain for broad views.

## Tips
- Arrive early for prime waterfront campsites.
- Carry a map showing designated sites; camping only allowed at marked locations.
- Bring a swimsuit—summer swimming is excellent.

## Warnings
- Frequent bears and raccoons—store food securely.
- Trails can flood after heavy rain; expect slick bog bridges.

## Bailouts
- Multiple spur trails return to the trailhead if you need to shorten the loop.

## Weather Considerations
- Mosquitoes and blackflies heavy in late spring; pack head net and repellent.
- Shoulder seasons can be chilly at night near the lake.
