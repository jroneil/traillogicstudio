# Katahdin Knife Edge Traverse

## Overview
Iconic point-to-point across Katahdin's Knife Edge from Pamola to Baxter Peak with airy exposure and unforgettable views.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
8.5 miles, ~3,900 feet gain.

## Difficulty
Very difficult; exposed scrambling with significant consequence—best for experienced parties and fair weather.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Ascend Helon Taylor to Pamola Peak, traverse the Knife Edge to Baxter Peak, then descend the Saddle or Hunt Trail.
- Start early to avoid afternoon wind; reservations required for parking.
- Carry a lightweight pack to maintain balance on the arête.

## Tips
- Gloves can help on sharp rocks.
- Move one at a time through the Chimney notch section.
- Check with rangers about current conditions before committing.

## Warnings
- Extremely exposed; avoid in high wind, rain, or thunderstorms.
- Loose rock and narrow sections demand deliberate footing.

## Bailouts
- After Baxter Peak, descend the Saddle for the fastest return to Chimney Pond.
- Helon Taylor is steep; retreating down it can be challenging—commit only with good weather.

## Weather Considerations
- Wind forecasts are critical; gusty days can make the traverse unsafe.
- Early fall can bring ice; traction may be required unexpectedly.
