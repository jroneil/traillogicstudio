# Presidential Range Day Skyline

## Overview
Ambitious single-day push across the Southern Presidentials from Mt. Madison to Mt. Pierce with nonstop alpine scenery and hut support.

## Distance + Gain (Approx)
18 miles, ~7,500 feet gain.

## Difficulty
Expert-level endurance hike with constant exposure and limited water above treeline.

## Itinerary Suggestions
- Spot a car at Crawford Notch and start predawn from Appalachia via Valley Way.
- Hit Madison and Adams early, refuel at Madison Hut, then traverse over Jefferson, Washington, Monroe, Eisenhower, and Pierce.
- Use the Crawford Path descent to end near the AMC Highland Center.

## Tips
- Travel light but carry layers; windchill can be significant even in summer.
- Time your Washington summit before mid-morning crowds.
- Consider using huts for quick water refills to save weight.

## Warnings
- Weather can become severe quickly; know how to retreat.
- Long stretches without natural water once on the ridge.

## Bailouts
- Gulfside connectors to the Great Gulf or Jewell Trails provide lower-elevation escape.
- Crawford Path offers the most straightforward exit once past Washington.

## Weather Considerations
- Check Mount Washington Observatory higher summits forecast.
- Thunderstorms and fog are common; visibility can drop rapidly.
